19.3 Cross-Validation of Moran's I Test and Regression Results

We performed a comprehensive cross-validation analysis comparing Moran's I spatial autocorrelation tests with differential gene expression (DE) analysis for imputation K=15 at the single cell level. This analysis helps validate the robustness of our findings across different statistical approaches.

Key Cross-Validation Findings

  • High Concordance: DE precision ranges from 96.9% to 100% across all conditions, indicating that nearly all genes identified as significant by DE are also identified by Moran's I
  • Method Complementarity: Moran's I identifies more genes overall (spatial autocorrelation), while DE is more selective but highly precise (temporal changes)
  • Lineage Consistency: Both CM and EM lineages show similar patterns of concordance, with EM lineage showing slightly higher Jaccard indices (0.78-0.81)
  • Modality Robustness: Results are consistent across all three modalities (K4, K27, RNAPII), with K27 showing the highest DE precision (100% in CM lineage)
